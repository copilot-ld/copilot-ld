Master Security Policy

TechNova Solutions
Comprehensive framework establishing security principles, requirements, and governance for all TechNova operations, systems, and data. Defines organizational security posture and mandates compliance with subordinate security policies.

Policy Scope

This policy applies to all TechNova employees, contractors, systems, data, and facilities. It establishes the foundation for information security across all divisions and mandates compliance with data protection, access control, and incident response requirements.

All subordinate policies must align with principles established in this master security policy. Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination of employment and legal action.

Core Security Principles

  • Confidentiality: Protect sensitive information from unauthorized access
  • Integrity: Ensure accuracy and completeness of data and systems
  • Availability: Maintain reliable access to systems and information
  • Least Privilege: Grant minimum necessary access rights
  • Defense in Depth: Implement multiple layers of security controls

Data Protection Policy

Establishes requirements for protecting sensitive data throughout its lifecycle including collection, storage, processing, transmission, and disposal. Mandates encryption, access controls, and audit logging for all protected data.

Data Classification

All data must be classified according to sensitivity level: Public, Internal, Confidential, or Restricted. Classification determines required security controls, retention requirements, and permissible uses.

Confidential data includes customer information, trade secrets, and proprietary business data. Restricted data includes payment card information, health records, and credentials. Both classifications require encryption at rest and in transit.

Customer Data Handling Procedures

Data Protection Policy
Detailed procedures for handling customer data including collection consent, storage requirements, access restrictions, retention periods, and deletion processes. Ensures compliance with privacy regulations and contractual obligations.

Collection and Consent

Customer data may only be collected with explicit informed consent. Privacy notices must clearly describe what data is collected, how it will be used, who it may be shared with, and how long it will be retained. Consent must be documented and retained.

Storage and Encryption

All customer data must be encrypted using AES-256 or equivalent when at rest. Database fields containing personally identifiable information require field-level encryption. Encryption keys must be managed through approved key management systems with rotation every 90 days.

Employee Data Guidelines

Data Protection Policy
Guidelines for collecting, storing, and processing employee personal information including HR records, performance data, and access logs. Balances business needs with employee privacy rights and regulatory requirements.

HR Data Access

Employee records are restricted to authorized HR personnel and direct managers for legitimate business purposes. Access is logged and audited quarterly. Employees have the right to review their own records and request corrections.

Access Control Policy

TechNova Solutions
Master Security Policy
Defines requirements for user authentication, authorization, and access management across all TechNova systems. Implements principle of least privilege and separation of duties.

Authentication Requirements

All system access requires strong authentication using multi-factor authentication (MFA) for any system containing confidential or restricted data. Passwords must meet complexity requirements: minimum 12 characters, mixed case, numbers, and symbols.

Single sign-on (SSO) must be used where available to reduce credential proliferation. Service accounts require approval and regular review. Shared credentials are prohibited.

Authentication Standards

Access Control Policy
Technical standards for implementing authentication mechanisms including password policies, MFA configuration, session management, and credential storage.

Multi-Factor Authentication

MFA must use time-based one-time passwords (TOTP), hardware tokens, or biometric factors. SMS-based MFA is permitted only as a backup method. Push notification authentication is acceptable if it includes number matching to prevent prompt fatigue attacks.

Authorization Matrix

Access Control Policy
Comprehensive matrix defining access rights for all systems and data based on job roles and responsibilities. Reviewed and updated quarterly to reflect organizational changes.

Role-Based Access

System access is granted based on predefined roles aligned with job functions. Role assignments require manager approval and are automatically provisioned through identity management systems. Emergency access procedures exist for critical business needs.

Incident Response Policy

TechNova Solutions
Master Security Policy
Establishes processes and responsibilities for detecting, responding to, and recovering from security incidents. Defines incident classification, escalation procedures, and post-incident analysis requirements.

Incident Classification

Security incidents are classified by severity: Critical (data breach, ransomware), High (system compromise, DDoS), Medium (malware detection, policy violation), Low (failed login attempts, suspicious activity). Severity determines response time and escalation path.

Security Breach Protocol

Incident Response Policy
Detailed protocol for responding to confirmed or suspected data breaches including containment, investigation, notification, and remediation procedures. Ensures compliance with breach notification laws.

Immediate Actions

Upon discovering a potential breach, immediately notify the security team and preserve evidence. Do not power off systems unless directed. Contain the breach by isolating affected systems while maintaining forensic integrity. Document all actions taken.

Recovery Procedures

Incident Response Policy
Step-by-step procedures for recovering from security incidents including system restoration, credential rotation, and validation of system integrity before returning to production.

System Restoration

Restore systems from known-good backups taken before the incident. Rebuild compromised systems from trusted sources. Apply all security patches. Rotate all credentials that may have been compromised. Conduct thorough security scan before reconnecting to production networks.
