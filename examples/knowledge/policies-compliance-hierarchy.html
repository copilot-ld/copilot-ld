BioNova Policy & Compliance Hierarchy

This corpus enumerates regulated operational, clinical, manufacturing, data governance, and quality risk management policy documents. Framework alignment references FDA, GMP, HIPAA, and ICH-GCP standards. Each policy includes rich descriptive text for hybrid retrieval and explicit hierarchical microdata relationships for multi-hop graph queries.

Compliance Frameworks

FDA Compliance Framework

Defines regulatory submission expectations, safety reporting cadence, oversight mechanisms, and dossier integrity checkpoints required under U.S. Food & Drug Administration guidelines.

GMP Compliance Framework

Establishes universal Good Manufacturing Practice expectations: batch record preservation, deviation escalation thresholds, contamination prevention barriers, and release verification protocols.

HIPAA Compliance Framework

Specifies protected health information handling, minimum necessary access, encryption in transit and at rest, audit tagging, and breach notification protocols under Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act mandates.

ICH-GCP Compliance Framework

Outlines international ethical and scientific quality standards for designing, conducting, recording, and reporting clinical trials that involve human participants.

Quality Management System

Master Quality Management System Policy

Central governance document defining integrated quality oversight for clinical development, manufacturing operations, data lifecycle, supplier assurance, and risk controls. Establishes unified escalation matrices, audit preparedness checkpoints, and cross-functional deviation triage workflows.

Subordinate Policies

Clinical Domain Policies

Clinical Trial Governance Policy

Defines protocol amendment controls, investigator oversight cadence, safety signal aggregation intervals, and interim analysis data lock standards for trial governance.

Patient Safety & Informed Consent Policy

Articulates global consent template stewardship, adverse event clarification guidelines, participant withdrawal processing, and risk communication transparency standards.

Clinical Data Integrity Policy

Specifies source data verification rates, audit trail immutability requirements, discrepancy management workflow, and blinded dataset segregation controls.

Data Governance Policies

Data Governance & Privacy Policy

Establishes classification taxonomy, retention thresholds, anonymization transformation pipeline standards, and stewardship accountability mappings.

Patient Data Protection Procedures

Defines encryption key rotation cadence, incident notification escalation flow, de-identification verification sampling, and secure archival lifecycle triggers.

Research Data Management Guidelines

Outlines FAIR principle alignment (findable, accessible, interoperable, reusable), experiment artifact versioning, lineage checkpoint metadata, and reproducibility documentation expectations.

Manufacturing & Quality Policies

Manufacturing Quality Policy

Defines statistical process control adoption scope, deviation categorization taxonomy, corrective and preventive action documentation, and continuous improvement cycle cadence.

GMP Compliance Procedures

Detail batch record authoring controls, supervised cleaning validation sampling, in-process specification adherence windows, and deviation escalation triggers.

Contamination Control Standards

Establishes environmental monitoring intervals, aseptic technique reinforcement protocols, material segregation zoning, and microbial excursion investigation criteria.

Batch Release Guidelines

Defines specification conformance verification, stability data snapshot requirements, quality batch disposition sign-off hierarchy, and exception risk assessment workflow.

Quality Risk Management Framework Policy

Outlines hazard identification brainstorming structure, probability/severity scoring matrices, mitigation plan lifecycle tracking, and periodic effectiveness reassessment cadence.

Supplier Qualification Procedures

Defines initial supplier audit checklist, performance KPI thresholds, corrective action collaboration workflow, and periodic requalification review triggers ensuring supply resilience.
